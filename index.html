naturalist approach

"old trees"

For the past few years I've been watching a gentleman on youtube named jon jondai. He is an authority on earthen building and being a general good steward of the earth. He emphasizes using natural materials found around you, preferring native varieties to production or import materials. A major goal of mine is to create food security for my family and our neighbors by nurturing native plants like edibles, ornimental plants, and with a bit of patience - hardwoods!

I mention all that to say - I want to plant some seeds with my coding. This blog page for one will be a great springboard for my ideas to live, a place for my self reporting and reflection of my coding practice to happen.

Speaking of coding practice - I will be spending the next day or two working through freeCodeCamps next offering: Intermediate CSS and a capstone project of Technical Page! I will link to all the pages as I get them wireframed and hosted on github.

took a L

"u win u lose"

Missed a post yesterday because I was working on Odin Project submission formatting and honestly havent established any regularity yet. Let this be a genuine attempt at routine for my new coding practice and for product upkeep.

When I was rock climbing more frequently I took yoga classes. In the classes the instructor used to emphasize building a practice that caters to your lifes needs, stressing that all of our bodies have immense networks of interconnectedness.

One such vast network I have been learning about recently is the Autonomic Nervous System "a component of the peripheral nervous system that regulates involuntary physiologic processes including heart rate, blood pressure, respiration, digestion, and sexual arousal."

Building a routine around coding and creating - a practice as my former instructor would say - is much like building routines around your ANS that can help improve depression, and more importantly for me, general physiological response to daily life.
